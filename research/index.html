Previous Works

I have worked on multiple topics throughout my studies such as dynamics and vibrations, instabilites, fracture mechanics, design and fabrications. During my PhD I have focused on the processes modelling of textile reinforcements by providing mathematical models, numerical solutions and experimental methods. To do this, I have analytically studied laminates in thermal environments under in-plane compressive forces, and have developed nonlinear material models to identify the material behavior during forming processes. For the experiments, I have designed various experimental rigs to model and determine the out-of-plane and bi-axial behavior of formable and tailorable materials.

Fractional Derivatives to Provide Improved Viscoelastic Model

The energy dissipation during forming may be rooted in various mechanisms. The viscous nature of composite material components imply that their mechanical behavior should be modeled based on the rate of forming. In this project, the viscoelastic behavior of a typical thermoplastic woven fabric was obtained experimentally, and is subsequently modelled based on fractional calculus. The results clearly showed that the viscoelastic moduli of the material, especially at lower temperature regimes, would be more accurately modeled through fractional time derivatives.

  • RT Faal, R Sourki, B Crawford, R Vaziri, AS Milani, Using Fractional Derivatives for Improved Viscoelastic Modeling of Textile Composites. Part II: Fabric under Different Temperatures, Composite Structures, 2020.

  • RT Faal, R Sourki, B Crawford, R Vaziri, AS Milani, Using fractional derivatives for improved viscoelastic modeling of textile composites. Part I: Fabric yarns, Journal of Composite Materials, 2020.

  • R Sourki, RT Faal, B Crawford, AS Milani, R Vaziri, Fractional Viscoelastic Behavior of Prepreg Woven Fabrics, Canadian International Conference On Composite Materials (CANCOM2019), Kelowna, BC, Canada, July 2019.


    • Vibration Analysis & Vibration Suppression of Structures: Structural Health Monitoring

    Vibration phenomena remains as one of the most important structural analysis and characterization method. This analysis shows its importance specifically in structural health monitoring applications. We have studied the vibration behavior of sturctures that are in severe conditions, e.g. in thermal environment, or having defects such as cracks. The results clearly showed that we can actively suppress the natural modes of structures and suppress the resonance of the structure and reduce the potential interior damage, i.e. edge cracks, interlaminar cracks and voids.

  • R Sourki, RT Faal, AS Milani, Vibration Analysis of Orthotropic FG Composite Plates in Thermal Environment Using HSDT: Frequency Suppression by Tuning the In-Plane Forces, Int. Journal of Applied and Computational Mathematics, 2020.

  • M Bastanfar, SA Hosseini, R Sourki, F Khosravi, Flexoelectric and surface effects on a cracked piezoelectric nanobeam: Analytical resonant frequency response, Archive of Mechanical Engineering, 2019.

  • R Sourki, SA Hosseini, Coupling effects of nonlocal and modified couple stress theories incorporating surface energy on analytical transverse vibration of a weakened nanobeam, The European Physical Journal Plus, 2017.

  • R Sourki, SA Hosseini, Free vibration analysis of size-dependent cracked microbeam based on the modified couple stress theory, Applied Physics A, 2016.
